    75 unique boy names with meanings and origin

    The comprehensive list of unique baby boy names with meaning goes back to the 19th century,peruses the old testaments and scours the universe for strong , cool and even nature-themed names- they're sure to find at least one you

    Written by Betsy Shaw | Nov 14, 2022

    • Old fashioned unique boy names
    • Cool unique boy names
    • Strong unique boy names
    • unique biblical boy names
    • Nature-Inspired unique biblical boy names

    Looking for a unique name for your baby boy? For our list, we only used names that didn't appear in the top 500 baby names in 2021, according to the U.S. Social Security Administration.

    Old-fashioned unique boy names

    Our old-fashioned, unique boy names didn't appear in the Social Security Administration's top 500 baby names in 2021, but they were in the top 100 names between 1879 and 1950.

    Clarence harkens back to Shakespearean times. It's thought to come from a Latin word meaning "bright and clear," though its origin could also be as an Irish name for someone who lived near the River Clare.

    Clyde is the name of a Scottish river and means "cleansing."

    Dewey is the Welsh form of the name David and means "beloved."

    Erasmus also means "beloved," though it derives from Greek. This name has never made the Social Security administration's top 1,000 list.

    Ernest comes from a German word meaning "serious." A popular name for boys in the late 1800s, its most notable bearer is the iconic American novelist Ernest Hemingway.

    Ernest comes from a German word meaning "serious." A popular name for boys in the late 1800s, its most notable bearer is the iconic American novelist Ernest Hemingway.

    Gordon is a Scottish name with uncertain origins. It could come from a Scottish surname meaning "spacious fort," though it could also be derived from names of regions in France, Belarus, and Spain.

    Lyle is a Scottish surname that means "island."

    Lloyd is a Welsh surname meaning "gray," which was coopted into a once-popular given name.

    Luther has a couple of origins. As a German name, it's a combination of the words liut, meaning "people," and heri, meaning "army." Luther also derives from a Greek name that means "free."

    Milton is an English name meaning "from the mill town."

    Murray is a surname with Scottish and Irish roots. It means "sea settlement."

    Rodney is thought to have originated as a surname for people from a region in England.

    Roy comes from an Old French word meaning "king"; it's also a variation of a Gaelic word that means "red."

    Virgil is an ancient name that's most recognized as that of the venerated Roman poet Publius Vergilius Maro (Virgil), writer of the epic poem Aeneid.

    Willis comes from William via the German name Wilhelm – meaning "protector."

    Thaddeus, as a variant of Theodore, means "God's gift." Another origin theory traces this name to the meaning of "courageous."

    Cool, unique boy names

    Alonzo is a variant of Alphonse. It's a Spanish name of German origin that means "noble and ready."

    Angus is an anglicized version of a Gaelic and Irish name meaning "one choice."

    Caius, an alternate version of the Latin name Gaius, is Julius Caesar's first name. All those vowels are one thing, but the strongest feature of this name is its meaning: "rejoice."

    Crosby is an English surname name that conjures an image of rural life. It comes from the word "cross" and is most likely a place name for people who lived at or near a junction.

    Crawford is another rural geographical name likely derived from a Scottish place. It breaks down to "crow ford."

    Dion is a Greek name that may mean "follower of Dionysus," the Greek god of wine and festivities. But it could also derive from Greek words mean "of Zeus" or "god."

    Duncan comes from Scottish and Irish Gaelic and translates as "dark-haired chief" or "brown noble."

    Eben is a shorter, softer variation of Ebenezer. From Hebrew, this name means "stone of help."

    Evander is an anglicized version of a Greek name meaning "good man."

    Garrison is a strong English surname meaning "son of Gerard," which itself is a Germanic name meaning "strong spear." While the surname and the noun are not one and the same, it's impossible not to also look to the word that means "fortified stronghold."

    Hopper is an English surname with a few origins: It could have been an occupational surname for someone who was a dancer, or it could have been the last name of someone who lived in a remote area surrounded by marsh.

    Leroy is a Norman word that means "the king."

    Kermit is a vintage name with Gaelic roots. It means "son of Dermot," an Irish given name that may mean "without restraint" or "without envy."

    Neo comes from the Greek word for "new."

    Roscoe is an English name that derives from Old Norse words meaning "doe wood" or "roebuck copse," referring to the roe deer.

    Rufus started out as an ancient Roman nickname and comes from the Latin word for the color red.

    Ryland is an English surname for someone who lived in an area where rye was grown.

    Uriah is a Hebrew name that means "God's light."

    Theron is an old name with tangled roots. Once a surname of Occitan origin – a region of Europe that encompasses parts of France, Spain, and Italy – it may also derive from the Greek word for "hunt."

    Turner is an English occupational name for a lathe worker. A lathe is a machine that used for woodturning and pottery shaping.

    Strong, unique boy names

    Ambrose has roots in a Latin word that means "immortal." Yes, it's related to ambrosia – the mythical food of the Greek gods.
